About

The approach

"I strive to tell a compelling story through both my words and music."

Ruben Plazier (Delft, 1998) is a classical pianist formed in the tradition of the Russian piano school. At fourteen he began at the Rotterdam Conservatory under Stéphane De May, who introduced him to French pianist Jean-Bernard Pommier — a meeting that would change his playing for good. After years of traveling between Rotterdam, Béziers and Paris, he was unanimously awarded his Diplôme Supérieur de Concertiste at the École Normale de Musique Alfred Cortot. As a soloist, he has performed concertos with orchestra and appeared in halls including the Concertgebouw Amsterdam and De Doelen Rotterdam, as well as in New York and Los Angeles. Beyond the stage, he curates the classical programming for De Doelen (2026/27 season) and Muziekgebouw Eindhoven, and performs as a duo with violinist Karen Su and as founder of the concert series Experience Klassiek.

Ruben Plazier was born in Delft in 1998 and began playing piano at six, on the instrument his parents kept at home. After his first lessons at the local music school in Dordrecht, a handful of lessons with Wibi Soerjadi at thirteen lit a spark that would set his course.

At fourteen, Ruben was admitted to the Rotterdam Conservatory, where he studied with the Belgian pianist Stéphane De May. Through De May, he met the French pianist Jean-Bernard Pommier — an encounter that would change everything. For three years, Ruben travelled to Béziers in the south of France to play for Pommier, while completing his Bachelor's degree with De May in Rotterdam.

When Pommier began teaching at the École Normale de Musique Alfred Cortot in Paris, Ruben started commuting weekly between Rotterdam and Paris. There, unanimously awarded by the jury each time, he earned his Diplôme Supérieur d'Enseignement, his Diplôme Supérieur d'Exécution, and in 2022, his Diplôme Supérieur de Concertiste. The close bond Ruben built with Pommier left a lasting mark on his playing: through him, the old Russian piano school was passed down, with its thinking on sound and phrasing.

As a soloist with orchestra, Ruben has performed concertos by composers including Rachmaninoff, Mendelssohn, Poulenc and Mozart. He has performed in halls including the Concertgebouw Amsterdam, De Doelen Rotterdam, TivoliVredenburg Utrecht, Muziekgebouw Eindhoven, Salle Cortot Paris, the Philharmonie Liège and the Rudolfinum in Prague, as well as in New York and Los Angeles. His awards include the international Festival d'Anniviers prize in Switzerland and first prize at the Archipel Music Competition, and he has taken masterclasses with, among others, Angela Hewitt, Frank Braley and Håkon Austbø.

Beyond performing, Ruben also works as a programmer: he curated the classical programming for De Doelen Rotterdam's 2026/27 season, and currently oversees the classical programming at Muziekgebouw Eindhoven.

Together with violinist Karen Su, he founded the concert series Experience Klassiek and its accompanying Academy, where he now guides the next generation of musicians — much as he himself was once guided.

Mentor

Jean-Bernard Pommier

Ruben met Jean-Bernard Pommier through his first teacher, Stéphane De May, at the Rotterdam Conservatory. What began as a few lessons in Béziers grew into one of the defining relationships of his musical life: years of travel, first to the south of France, later weekly to Paris, for lessons that stretched into long conversations over breakfast, dinner, and late into the night. Pommier demanded a willingness to start over, again and again, and gave in return something that reached far beyond technique: a way of listening, of thinking about sound, that he himself had inherited from generations of great pedagogues within the Russian piano school. That inheritance now sits at the core of how Ruben plays, and teaches.

Solo

Recitals

Full-length programmes centred on French repertoire and the Russian pianistic lineage — Debussy, Scriabin, and composers just outside the standard canon.

Duo

With Karen Su

A working partnership built over years of shared repertoire, from sonata literature to newly paired programmes for smaller, more intimate halls.

Trio

With Karen Su & Luc Tooten

A trio programme built around Karen Su on violin and Luc Tooten on cello, pairing chamber repertoire with the same attention to line and story as the solo and duo work.
